# PROJECT_BRIEF.md

## Project
PetLinkID

## Purpose
PetLinkID is an iOS-first app designed to help pet owners manage trusted pet identity information and pet passport-style records in one place.

## Product vision
Create a simple, trustworthy and premium mobile experience that helps pet owners store, access and manage important pet information without friction.

## Core problem being solved
Pet information is often fragmented across paper records, vet documents, notes, emails and photos. PetLinkID aims to bring these records together into a single structured digital experience.

## Target users
- Pet owners who want a clear and reliable profile for each pet
- Users who need quick access to pet information, records or identity details
- Future partner or service workflows where verified pet information may be useful

## Product goals
- Make pet profiles easy to create and maintain
- Make important pet information easy to retrieve on mobile
- Build trust through a clean UX and reliable data handling
- Prepare the app for production release on iOS
- Establish a foundation for future passport, verification or sharing features

## Current priorities
- Stabilise the app architecture and current user flows
- Ensure authentication and session handling are reliable
- Confirm clean environment separation across development, staging and production
- Improve iOS build and release readiness
- Clarify the roadmap for data model, storage, onboarding and account flows

## Non-negotiables
- Production-minded implementation, not throwaway prototype work
- Clear and trustworthy UX
- Safe handling of user data
- No hardcoded secrets or fragile configuration
- Maintainable codebase that supports iteration and release

## Likely functional areas
- User authentication and account management
- Pet profile creation and editing
- Pet record and passport-style data capture
- Media or document storage
- Mobile-first onboarding and profile flows
- Settings, account, and support or help content

## Success measures
- Users can reliably sign in and stay signed in
- Users can create and manage pet profiles without confusion
- Key records load quickly and accurately
- The iOS app can be built, tested and prepared for App Store release
- The codebase is stable enough for safe iterative development

## Working assumptions to confirm
These are reasonable working assumptions based on prior context and should be updated once confirmed in the repo:
- Frontend stack uses React, TypeScript, Vite and Tailwind
- Backend services are supported by Supabase
- Mobile packaging uses Capacitor for iOS
- The immediate release focus is iOS before broader platform expansion
- The app is moving from prototype stage toward production hardening
